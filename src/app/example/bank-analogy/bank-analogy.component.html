Example: NgRx Store Concept — A Bank Analogy

Think of the NgRx Store like a Bank.

The Store is the Bank — a central, secure place that holds your data (like the bank holds your money).

The State in the store is like the account balance — it contains your information.

Multiple customers (or parts of your application) can interact with the bank, but no one can directly take or put money into it.

Even though it's your money, you cannot directly credit or debit your account. You must follow specific procedures like:

  • Filling a deposit form or
  • Submitting a withdrawal request

These forms are like Actions in NgRx.

And just like the bank has a teller or a system to process your request, NgRx has a Reducer that handles the action and updates the state immutably — meaning it creates a new version of the state, rather than modifying it directly.
